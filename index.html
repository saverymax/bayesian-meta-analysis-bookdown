1 Technical preface

This is the bookdown report for my meta-analysis of ivermectin as a treatment for COVID-19. This was originally intended as my course project for the meta-analysis course from KU Leuven. The report is hosted at https://saverymax.github.io/bayesian-meta-analysis-bookdown/

To locally render the book, run

install.packages("bookdown")
bookdown::render_book('index.Rmd', 'bookdown::pdf_book')

This report reviews Bayesian methods for estimating random effect models for meta-analysis, with a particular focus on the effect of prior information. Then, using summary data from studies conducted to measure the effect of ivermectin as a treatment for COVID-19, we demonstrate the use of Bayesian methods for meta-analysis, experimenting with the effect of different priors on the between-study variance.

The summary data for ivermectin is provided in a blog post by the statistician Andrew Gelman . In the post, Gelman reconstructs a meta-analysis on the effect of ivermectin. He demonstrates how the selection of the prior for the between-study variance can influence conclusions of the meta-analysis, in this case the extent to which ivermectin has a positive effect on treating COVID-19. We take his discussion of this topic as a starting point for this report. Given that there was a large amount of misinformation regarding ivermectin and various pseudo-scientific studies regarding this topic , it is quite an interesting case study of the role that Bayesian modelling can play in meta-analyses.
